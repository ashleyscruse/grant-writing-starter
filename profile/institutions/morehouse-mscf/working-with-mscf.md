# Working with MSCF

The human process for bringing MSCF into a proposal. The AI can walk the researcher through these steps and draft the requests; the researcher does the outreach.

## What MSCF can provide for a proposal

- Computational resources to name in Facilities, Equipment, and Other Resources.
- Data storage and transfer language for the Data Management Plan.
- A compute estimate or scaling statement to support feasibility.
- Where appropriate and allowed, a Letter of Collaboration (see `letters-when-and-how.md`).
- Training and program ties for Broader Impacts. `[REFINE]`

## Steps and lead times

1. **Decide what you need** (named compute only, a collaboration, a compute estimate, training tie). Most proposals need only the Facilities and DMP language, which require no sign-off.
2. **If you need a compute estimate or a letter, contact MSCF early.** `[REFINE: who to email, and how far before the deadline]`
3. **Provide them** your project title, the program, the deadline, and a short description so they can tailor anything they sign.
4. **Confirm allocation framing** for the specific program before you submit. `[REFINE]`
