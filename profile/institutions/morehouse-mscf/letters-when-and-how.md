# Letters: when, and how (NSF)

Before drafting any letter, the AI must check the solicitation (via `solicitation-review`). Letters are program-specific, and the wrong kind can get a proposal returned without review.

## The distinction (NSF)

- **Letter of Support** (substantive endorsement): generally **not permitted** in standard NSF proposals unless the solicitation explicitly requests it. Do not add one otherwise.
- **Letter of Collaboration** (for an actual collaborator): allowed, but NSF mandates a **near-verbatim format**. Anything beyond it can be flagged. Use this only when MSCF (or a person) is a genuine collaborator on the project, not merely a compute provider.

Naming MSCF compute in **Facilities, Equipment, and Other Resources** needs no letter at all and is the usual path. See `facilities-and-resources.md`.

## NSF Letter of Collaboration format

Use NSF's required wording (confirm against the current PAPPG before submitting):

> "If the proposal submitted by Dr. [PI] entitled [Project Title] is selected for funding by NSF, it is my intent to collaborate and/or commit resources as detailed in the Project Description or the Facilities, Equipment and Other Resources section of the proposal."

On the collaborator's letterhead, signed and dated. Do not add extra praise or detail; that is what makes it noncompliant.

> DRAFT. Confirm against the current NSF PAPPG, and check the specific solicitation, before using.
